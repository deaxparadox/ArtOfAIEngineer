# Phase 1 — ML Foundations

> **The bedrock.** You cannot debug an LLM hallucination, tune a RAG pipeline, or evaluate a fine-tuned model without
> understanding what's actually happening underneath. This phase gives you that foundation.

---

## Why This Phase Matters

Every AI engineer eventually hits a moment where something breaks in a way that defies the tutorial explanation.
The model confidently gives wrong answers. The loss curve looks right but the model doesn't generalize. A prompt
change improves one metric and tanks another.

The people who debug these fastest aren't the ones who know the most frameworks. They're the ones who understand
the fundamentals — what the model is actually optimizing for, why overfitting happens at a structural level, what
"convergence" actually means.

This phase builds that mental model. It's not glamorous, but skipping it makes every subsequent phase harder.

## Prerequisites for This Phase

- Python basics (functions, loops, data structures)
- High school math: what a function is, what a derivative means conceptually
- No prior ML experience required

---

## What You'll Be Able To Do After This Phase

- Explain what ML is actually doing (not just "it learns from data")
- Trace why a model is or isn't learning from a loss curve
- Choose an optimizer and defend your choice
- Diagnose overfitting vs underfitting and know which fix to apply
- Explain the architectural leap from classical ML to deep learning
